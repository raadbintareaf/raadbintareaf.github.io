Biography & Focus

I am a Professor of AI and Data Science at XU Exponential University of Applied Sciences in Potsdam, Germany. My research and academic pedagogy are positioned at the intersection of deep learning interpretability, distributed systems, and real-world impact. I specialize in Large Language Models (LLMs), Explainable AI (XAI), and applied machine learning.

Beyond academia, I maintain active collaborations with the public sector and industry to deploy machine learning frameworks that address critical challenges. In my independent research, I explore algorithmic trading mechanisms, the integration of Web 3.0 technologies, and the fine-tuning of LLMs utilizing cybersecurity and SIEM data sets to enhance autonomous threat detection.

Funded Research & Major Projects

XAI for Medical Health

Collaboration: Abu Dhabi University, Zayed University, MIT

Developing Explainable AI (XAI) frameworks tailored for medical diagnostics. This research focuses on utilizing methodologies like SHAP, LIME, and Grad-CAM to ensure that complex AI inferences derived from clinical and cardiovascular data are transparent, legally compliant, and trustworthy for healthcare professionals.

Healthcare AIInterpretability

FAIRWORK Germany

Collaboration: WZB Berlin, University of Oxford

An international action-research project examining the digital labor economy. We utilize computational and behavioral analytics to assess platform work conditions against global fair work principles, pushing for regulatory resilience and algorithmic fairness in the gig economy.

Social ComputingAlgorithmic Fairness

RITS (Resilient Infrastructure)

Brandenburg Model Region Initiative

The Resilient Infrastructure Technology Suite explores the operational nexus of energy, water, and food grids. We deploy digital twins, DePIN (Decentralized Physical Infrastructure Networks), and Green Swarming IoT combined with AI to create self-sustaining, fault-tolerant regional infrastructure.

Digital TwinsDePINIoT

QAVCM

Voluntary Carbon Market Analytics

Designing a high-fidelity analytics system for the quantitative, AI-assisted, and real-time evaluation of voluntary carbon market (VCM) CO₂ certificates. This ensures higher transparency, tracks ecological impact verification, and mitigates greenwashing using real-time data ingestion.

Core Research Domains

  • Large Language Models & RAG: Architectural optimization, retrieval-augmented generation pipelines, and domain-specific fine-tuning (focusing on cybersecurity and SIEM integration).
  • Explainable AI (XAI): Bridging the gap between model accuracy and human interpretability in high-stakes environments like health, finance, and the public sector.
  • Social Computing: Behavioral analytics, sentiment mining, and measuring the societal impact of algorithmic deployment.
  • Decentralized Systems: Blockchain applications, Web 3.0 infrastructure, and resilient digital twin modeling for critical physical resources.
